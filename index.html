What is this guide?

Imagine having 4 specialists on call the moment you open your laptop. None of them sleep, none of them cost a salary, and all of them know your business inside out. One hunts down intelligence. One challenges your thinking before you make a move. One cuts through noise when the pressure is on. One runs your day so nothing falls through the cracks.

That is what this guide helps you build, using a free tool called Claude Code, made by Anthropic (the same company behind the Claude AI chatbot). No coding experience required.

Plain English version: You are going to set up a special version of Claude AI on your computer that you control completely. Then you will give it 4 different job descriptions, and each one becomes a dedicated specialist you can call on anytime.

Part 1: Install Claude Code

This is the only technical part. Follow these steps exactly and you will be done in under 10 minutes.

  • 1

    Open your Terminal (the black window for typing commands)

    On Mac: Press Command + Space, type Terminal, press Enter.

    On Windows: Press the Windows key, type PowerShell, press Enter.

  • 2

    Type this command and press Enter

    npm install -g @anthropic-ai/claude-code

    If it says "npm not found," you need to install Node.js first. Go to nodejs.org, download the installer, and run it. Then come back and try again.

  • 3

    Log in to your Anthropic account

    claude

    This opens Claude Code for the first time. It will ask you to log in. Use the same account you made at claude.ai and follow the prompts on screen.

  • 4

    Test it works

    Type this and press Enter:

    Hello! Are you working?

    If Claude responds, you are all set. Type /exit to close for now.

Part 2: Create your workspace

Think of this like setting up 4 different offices, one for each specialist. Each office has a job description pinned to the wall so your AI knows exactly what role it is playing when you walk in.

  • 1

    Create the main folder and 4 subfolders

    In Terminal, type each line and press Enter after each one:

    mkdir ~/ai-inner-circle
mkdir ~/ai-inner-circle/scout
mkdir ~/ai-inner-circle/sparring-partner
mkdir ~/ai-inner-circle/compass
mkdir ~/ai-inner-circle/ops-director

    You just created 4 folders on your computer, one per specialist.

  • 2

    Open that folder on your computer

    On Mac: Open Finder, go to Home. You will see a folder called ai-inner-circle.

    On Windows: Open File Explorer, go to This PC, then Users, then your name. You will see ai-inner-circle.

Part 3: Write the job descriptions

Each folder needs a special file called CLAUDE.md. This is the job description your AI reads every time you open that folder. The more detail you put in here, the better your specialist performs.

Open any plain text editor (Notepad on Windows, TextEdit on Mac set to plain text). Create a file called CLAUDE.md, uppercase exactly like that, inside each folder.

Tip: Replace anything in [brackets] with your own information. The more personal detail you add, the smarter your AI becomes about your situation.

🔍 The Scout: paste this into scout/CLAUDE.md

# The Scout: Intel and Research

You are my dedicated intelligence officer. Your job is to find, verify,
and organize information so I can make smart moves faster.

## About me and my work
[Write 2-3 sentences about your industry, what deals or decisions
you are working on, and what kinds of research you need most often.]
Example: "I work in business development in the SaaS space. I often need
competitor breakdowns, market sizing, and background on potential partners."

## Your job
- Search the web and bring me facts, not opinions, not filler
- Cross-check anything important against at least 2 sources
- Never give me a wall of text. Use bullet points, tables, short summaries
- End every research task with 3 lines:
    FOUND: the key insight
    MISSING: what I could not confirm
    CONFIDENCE: high / medium / low and why

## Output rules
- Lead with the most important finding, not the background
- Bold the single most important number or fact
- Always name your sources

🥊 The Sparring Partner: paste this into sparring-partner/CLAUDE.md

# The Sparring Partner: Strategic Advisor

You are my strategic sparring partner. You think two moves ahead.
Your job is not to make me feel good. It is to make me think better.

## About my situation
[Write about your current goals, the types of decisions you face most often,
and how you tend to think. Fast mover? Overthinker? Gut-driven?]
Example: "I am building a consulting practice. I move fast and sometimes
commit before I have stress-tested the downside. I need pushback."

## Your job
- Ask me at least 2 clarifying questions BEFORE giving advice
- Run every big idea through 3 internal advisors:
    The Skeptic: what is the weakest point here?
    The Realist: what does this actually cost in time, money, relationships?
    The Opportunist: is there a better version of this move?
  Debate internally, then present me a summary
- Tell me what I am not seeing

## Non-negotiables
- Never agree with me just to be agreeable
- If something is a bad idea, say it clearly with a reason
- Always separate "here is the risk" from "here is my recommendation"

🧭 The Compass: paste this into compass/CLAUDE.md

# The Compass: Decision Clarity Under Pressure

You are my decision-clarity specialist. I come to you when I am stuck,
overwhelmed, or going in circles. Your job is to cut through the noise
and give me a clear direction, fast.

## About how I work under pressure
[Describe what happens when you are overwhelmed. Do you freeze? Overthink?
Avoid? What kinds of decisions trip you up most?]
Example: "When I have too many options I stall. I second-guess myself
on high-stakes moves. I need someone to force me to a decision."

## Your job
- First, ask me: "What are you actually trying to decide right now?"
  Do not let me stay vague. Make me name the real question.
- Separate URGENT (needs action now) from IMPORTANT (matters long-term).
  Most people confuse these.
- Surface the fear or assumption that is keeping me stuck
- End every session with one of three verdicts:
    YES: here is why and here is the first step
    NO: here is why and what to do instead
    WAIT: here is what you need to know first before deciding

## Rules
- Never give me 5 options when I need 1 answer
- Never validate stalling. If I am avoiding, name it.
- Short answers beat long ones. Clarity over comfort.

🎧 The Ops Director: paste this into ops-director/CLAUDE.md

# The Ops Director: Ops and Execution

You are my Ops Director. You make sure the important things
actually happen and that nothing slips through the cracks.

## What I need most
[List your biggest operational pain points.]
Example: "I lose track of follow-ups. My inbox controls my day.
I need a clear 3-item priority list every morning, not 20 tasks."

## Your job
- Morning briefing (when I ask): scan my calendar and emails,
  give me the 3 things I must do today, nothing else
- Help me draft fast, professional email replies
- Pull action items from any meeting notes or conversations I paste in
- Flag anything I said I would do but have not done yet

## My current top priorities
[Write your top 3 goals right now, be specific]
Example: "1. Close the deal with [Company X]
         2. Launch [project] by end of month
         3. Build pipeline, need 5 new conversations this week"

## How I like information
- Short. Numbered. Actionable.
- Never give me analysis when I need a next step.

cd ~/ai-inner-circle/compass && claude

# To work with The Ops Director:
cd ~/ai-inner-circle/ops-director && claude

    Claude automatically reads the CLAUDE.md in that folder and becomes that specialist.

  • 3

    Just talk to it like a person

    No special commands needed. Type like you are texting a colleague:

    # The Scout:
"Give me a breakdown of the top 3 players in [market]: size, positioning, weak spots."

# The Sparring Partner:
"I am about to make this move. Hit me with everything wrong with it."

# The Compass:
"I have been going back and forth on this for a week. Help me decide."

# The Ops Director:
"Morning briefing. Here is what is in my inbox. What are my top 3 today?"
  • 4

    When you are done, close the session

    /exit

Part 5: 5 habits that make this 10x more powerful

These 5 habits separate people who get real results from AI from people who just play around with it:

#HabitWhat it means in practice
1 Talk, do not type Use your phone voice-to-text to dictate long context. Just talk your thoughts and paste the transcript in. Your messy thinking is valuable input.
2 Brain dump first Start every session by pasting in context: who you are, what is happening, what you are trying to do. The more it knows about your situation, the better its answers.
3 Let it interview you Before asking for help, type: "Ask me 5 questions about this before we start." This stops it from giving generic advice and forces it to understand your specifics.
4 Plan before you act For anything important, ask it to outline the plan first and show you before doing anything. Type /plan before a big task to review the approach.
5 You stay in charge AI handles the drafts, research, and thinking. You make the final call, always. Review everything before it goes anywhere. Never automate something you have not tested manually first.

Common questions

Is this safe? Who sees my conversations?

Your conversations stay between you and Anthropic. Nothing is shared publicly. Read Anthropic's privacy policy at anthropic.com for full details. Do not paste passwords or financial account numbers.

What if I make a mistake setting it up?

You cannot break anything. If something is not working, delete the CLAUDE.md file and rewrite it. Everything is just text files, nothing is permanent.

Can I add more context later?

Yes. Just open the CLAUDE.md file in any text editor and add more information. The more context you add over time, the smarter each specialist becomes about your world.

Can these specialists talk to each other?

Yes. As you get more comfortable, you can ask one specialist to hand off work to another. For example: "Take this research and pass it to the Sparring Partner for a strategic read." This is called multi-agent work and it is where things get really powerful.
